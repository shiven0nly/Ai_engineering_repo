# Insights from India Air Quality Analysis

## Objective

The objective of this project was to analyze India's daily air quality data, identify pollution patterns, understand relationships between pollutants, and derive meaningful insights through Exploratory Data Analysis.

---

## Major Findings

### 1. Air Quality Distribution

- Most observations belong to the Moderate and Satisfactory AQI categories.

- The AQI distribution is positively skewed due to occasional severe pollution events.

---

### 2. City-wise Pollution

- Ahmedabad recorded the highest average AQI.

- Delhi contributed the highest number of observations.

- Several northern Indian cities consistently experienced poorer air quality than southern cities.

---

### 3. Seasonal Trends

- Air quality deteriorates during winter.

- Monsoon months consistently show lower AQI because rainfall removes airborne pollutants.

---

### 4. Pollutant Behaviour

- PM2.5 has the strongest relationship with AQI.

- PM10 shows the highest average concentration.

- NO2 and NOx are strongly correlated.

---

### 5. Correlation Analysis

- PM2.5 and PM10 are the most influential pollutants affecting AQI.

- Vehicular pollutants exhibit strong positive relationships with each other.

---

## Conclusion

The analysis indicates that particulate matter (especially PM2.5 and PM10) plays a dominant role in determining air quality across Indian cities. Seasonal weather patterns significantly influence pollution levels, with winter experiencing the poorest air quality and monsoon providing noticeable improvements.
